Blood Donation Tips

Essential guidelines to ensure a safe and successful blood donation

Donation Timeline

Days Before Donation
  • Get adequate sleep
  • Eat iron-rich foods
  • Stay hydrated
  • Avoid fatty foods
Day of Donation
  • Eat a light meal
  • Wear comfortable clothing
  • Bring ID and donor card
  • Avoid strenuous activities
After Donation
  • Rest for 10-15 minutes
  • Have refreshments
  • Keep bandage on
  • Avoid heavy lifting

Do's

Nutrition
  • Eat iron-rich foods (spinach, lean meats)
  • Have a light meal before donation
  • Drink plenty of water
  • Include vitamin C rich foods
Rest
  • Get at least 7 hours of sleep
  • Avoid strenuous activities before donation
  • Take breaks when needed
Preparation
  • Bring valid identification
  • Wear comfortable clothing
  • Schedule donation at convenient time

Don'ts

Avoid
  • Fatty foods before donation
  • Alcohol for 24 hours before
  • Smoking on donation day
  • Heavy meals right before
Activities
  • Strenuous exercise before donation
  • Heavy lifting after donation
  • Operating heavy machinery immediately after
Health
  • Donate if feeling unwell
  • Skip meals before donation
  • Hide medical conditions

After Donation Care

Recovery Time

Rest for 10-15 minutes after donation. Your body will replace the fluid within 24 hours.

Hydration

Drink extra fluids for the next 24-48 hours to help your body recover.

Activity

Avoid strenuous physical activity for the rest of the day after donating.

Bandage

Keep the bandage on for at least 4-5 hours after donation.

Benefits of Regular Blood Donation

Heart Health

Regular donation can reduce the risk of heart attacks and liver ailments.

Calorie Burn

Donating blood burns approximately 650 calories per donation.

New Blood Cells

Stimulates the production of new blood cells, improving overall health.
